# Business Strategy

## Use When
Keywords: "GTM", "go-to-market", "business case", "competitive", "market sizing", "TAM", "pricing strategy", "unit economics", "pitch", "positioning", "business model", "market entry"

## Output Standards
- **Number everything**: "5 channels ranked by CAC" not "several channels"
- **Source every figure**: State assumption basis for every market size number
- **Multiple scenarios**: Best/base/worst on all projections. Black swan for major bets.
- **Red flags explicitly**: 2-3 assumptions that, if wrong, kill the thesis — state them
- **Anti-hype**: No "revolutionary", "disrupting" without evidence. Quantify or cut.

## 5 Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **TAM: always top-down AND bottom-up.** Show both. If they diverge >2x, explain why. Use bottom-up as primary.

2. **GTM: name the beachhead ICP before any channel discussion.** No ICP = no valid GTM. Force segment → channel, never channel → segment.

3. **Competitive: include "do nothing" and "current workaround" as alternatives.** Not just direct competitors. What does the customer actually do today?

4. **Business case structure:** Problem (quantified) → Solution (mechanism, not marketing) → Investment (people+money+time) → Return (revenue+cost avoidance) → Risk register (3+ risks with mitigations). Always this order.

5. **Pricing: include expansion revenue path.** A flat fee with no growth lever is a ceiling, not a strategy. Show: initial price → what drives upsell → year 2 ARPU vs year 1.

## Key Templates (use on request)

- **TAM/SAM/SOM**: top-down + bottom-up reconciliation table
- **GTM channels**: CAC estimate + payback + scalability ceiling per channel
- **Competitive matrix**: price | target segment | core moat | key weakness | recent moves
- **Unit economics**: CAC | LTV | LTV:CAC | payback period | gross margin | burn multiple
- **Risk register**: Risk | Probability (1-5) | Impact (1-5) | Score | Mitigation | Owner

## Role Anchoring

For strategy and financial analysis, use: "Senior strategy consultant. McKinsey/Bain quality bar. Cite sources. Multiple scenarios. State assumptions explicitly."
